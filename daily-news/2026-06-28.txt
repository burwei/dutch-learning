BBC: Infantino made 27 flights by private jet during the World Cup
FIFA president Gianni Infantino made at least 27 flights by private jet during the group stage of the football World Cup in North America.
The BBC reports this based on an analysis of the flight movements of an aircraft linked to the world football association.
According to the BBC, Infantino attended 24 matches in sixteen days across the United States, Canada and Mexico.
In doing so, the plane is said to have covered more than 50,000 kilometres and spent over 66 hours in the air.
The longest flight was from Vancouver to Miami, a distance of more than 4,500 kilometres.
On some days the aircraft made three flights, so the Swiss FIFA president could attend several matches in one day.
The BBC estimates that during this period the aircraft emitted roughly as much CO2 as the average annual emissions of 78 people worldwide.
The calculation is based on the estimated fuel consumption of a Gulfstream G650ER, the type of aircraft Infantino is assumed to have travelled on.
The exact emissions cannot be determined, partly because it is unknown how many passengers were on board and how much fuel the aircraft actually used.
In a response to the BBC, FIFA did not confirm that Infantino travelled on the aircraft in question.
The world football association did say that the president "travels regularly for work and tournament-related obligations".
According to FIFA, this sometimes involves scheduled flights and sometimes a chartered private plane, depending on what is most efficient and cost-effective.
The association gave no answer to additional questions from the BBC, including about the number of occupants and any offsetting of the emissions.
Climate organisations call the intensive use of a private jet hard to reconcile with FIFA's sustainability ambitions.
The association has set itself the goal of halving its emissions by 2030 and being climate-neutral by 2040.
The 2026 World Cup will be played in sixteen host cities spread across the United States, Canada and Mexico.
As a result, the travel distances between matches are much greater than during the 2022 World Cup in Qatar, where all the stadiums lay relatively close together.
